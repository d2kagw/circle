Circle

  • Items and Events can be played on any player including yourself
  • When you play a politics card on another player, you can change their political status
  • The game starts with no global events shown, as you play global events you create two piles. There can only ever be two cards shown. When a global event is covered it's effects no longer have impact.
  • Game setup:
    1. Separate the character, state, goal and game cards
    2. Shuffle the goal and game cards
    3. The dealer assigns characters to players
    4. The dealer gives each player a political and relationship state card. The player chooses their start state
    5. The dealer gives each player 5 game cards each. Keep these hidden
    6. The dealer gives each player 3 goal cards, the players keep 2. The goals remain secret - only you know what you're trying to achieve
    7. The owner of the game starts
  • A turn involves playing two cards from your hand. A play can be:
    • Play an event card by playing it on yourself, another player, or if it's a global event, you place it in one of two piles in the middle of the table. Global Events, or events played on other players take effect as soon as you've finished your turn.
    • Play an item on yourself by placing it next to your character card. Or play the item on someone else, by placing it next to their character card and action any effects that might take place
    • Put a card you don't want by putting it in the discard pile
    You need to have 5 (or more) cards in your hand at the end of your turn, so draw the required number of cards from the draw pile at the end of your turn.
  • If the draw pile is exhausted, shuffle the discard pile and keep going
  • To win the game, you need to reach all of the goals outlined by your goal cards. Simply shout loudly and make a scene then force your friends to buy you a congratulatory beer
